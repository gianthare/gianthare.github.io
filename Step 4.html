    • Step 4. Molding & Casting

    December 8, 2017 by Sungeun

    Molding and casting is like making a dozens of ice cubes from ice cube molds.

    The most important thing is EXPERIENCE!!, said Rodrigo. Almost every information of how to mold and cast is found in the website below.

    For this process, several things are needed such as an electronic scale, cups and sticks to mix two kinds of rubbers, and vinyl to cover the table. Obviously the safety measure is very important. We had to put on two layers gloves and a mask during the time. The specific safety data is also on the site.

    [MOLDING]
    1. spray the oil to a frame and an object in order to take the mold out of the object and the frame.
    2. weigh the rubbers. Then mix the rubbers thoroughly and pour it in the frame within 6min.
    3. after 30min, take out the mold from the frame. Tah dahhh

    [CASTING]
    1. weigh the rubbers. Then mix the rubbers thoroughly and pour it in the frame within 7min.
    2. after 60min, take out the cast from the frame. Tah dahhh

    “Mold Star® rubbers are easy to use platinum silicones which are mixed 1A:1B by volume (no weighing scale necessary). Mold Star® silicones feature relatively low viscosities and vacuum degassing is not required for most applications. Mold Star® 16 FAST is a fast material with a 6 minute pot life and 30 minute cure time.”
    몰딩: https://www.smooth-on.com/products/mold-star-16-fast/

    I made one Lego piece, but I was too fast in pouring the mold and cast rubbers that it had a lot of bubbles in it. Especially as the cast mix is not that thick as the mold mix, I should pour at a much slower rate.
